Where it started

Growing up, I was raised a Catholic because that what my whole family was. I would only go to church because I felt like that was something that I had to do. Also, because my mom made me go to church. I have always believed that God existed and I still do. But I never felt "good" about being Catholic.

The journey

Once I was older, I was able to have my own opinion and voice in the matter. Finally, I mentioned something to my mom about it, and she felt the same way. So that is when we began to search for a new church. We didn't know exactly what we were searching for but just hoped that we would find it. This process lasted for a few months to a year. Towards the end, we began to give up on our search. So we took a break.

Hope

One day, my brother, Mario, mentioned to us that one of his friends had invited him to a church. Mario and my other brother, Anthony, attended a church called The Caring Place for the first time by themselves. Once they returned, they said that they liked it, but it had a very different feel than what we were used to. My mom and I were kind of nervous to attend because we weren't sure what to expect. The next weekend, we went and really enjoyed the sermon.

Our new home

Fast forward to present day, my family and I still attend the Caring Place every Sunday and we have seen our faith grow tremendously at this church, that we now call home.
